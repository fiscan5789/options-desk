About this scanner

This scanner implements a programmatic version of the Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP) as described by Mark Minervini in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard, combined with his Trend Template qualifying criteria. It runs in both bullish (long breakout) and bearish (short breakdown) modes.

Trend Template (gating): A stock must satisfy ALL criteria to enter scoring. Bull version requires price > 150-day & 200-day MAs, 150-day > 200-day, 200-day rising for at least 20 bars, 50 > 150 > 200 MA stacking, price within 25% of 52-week high, price at least 30% above 52-week low, and 6-month relative strength rank in the top 30% of the scan universe. Bear version inverts each criterion.

VCP pattern detection: The scanner identifies contractions as N% pullbacks from local highs (or rallies from local lows for bear). A valid base requires 2+ progressively smaller contractions over 5-65 trading days, with volume contracting through the base. The pivot point is the high of the most recent contraction (or low for bear) — this is the breakout/breakdown trigger price.

Two phases: "Firing" = price has broken the pivot on the most recent bar with volume confirmation. "Forming" = base is set up and price is within a defined distance of the pivot, waiting to trigger.

Composite score (0-100): Base points by stage (40 for firing today, 25 for firing 1-2 bars ago, 20 for forming within 3% of pivot, 10 for deeper-in-base), plus pattern quality modifiers (more contractions, tighter final contraction, declining volume), trend context (RS rank, distance from highs, MA slope), breakout volume quality, and market regime alignment.

Options selection (Top 10 firing picks per direction): 14-28 DTE for firing breakouts (faster moves than TD reversals), 28-45 DTE for forming setups (haven't triggered yet). Same 3-strike slate as TD scanner (ITM/ATM/OTM by delta).

Earnings filter: When enabled, the scanner queries Polygon's /vX/reference/financials endpoint for each trend-template-passing candidate and excludes any whose most recent 10-Q/10-K filing was more than the threshold (default 45 days) ago. VCP trades typically hold for 2-6 weeks, so the threshold is more conservative than the TD scanner's 70 days — if a stock filed 45+ days ago, its next earnings likely falls inside your hold window. Filing date is a proxy for the earnings announcement date (typically within a week, but not identical). Q4 results often fold into the annual 10-K which extends the gap to 4-5 months. Always verify earnings dates in your broker's calendar before trading. Earnings dates are cached in browser localStorage for 7 days to minimize API calls.

Important caveats: Algorithmic VCP detection is heuristic, not deterministic. The scanner will surface candidates that don't look like clean VCPs on visual inspection — chart-check every top pick before trading. The trend template is conservative — many valid VCPs from the Minervini playbook will be filtered out by strict template enforcement. This is a candidate-narrowing tool, not a signal-generation tool. Options data is end-of-day from Polygon — always verify live prices in your broker.
