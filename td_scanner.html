About this scanner

This tool implements TD Sequential as defined in DeMark's standard published rules. A scan runs against the most recently closed daily bar — if you scan during market hours, today's partial bar is excluded.

Setup phase (9): bullish (buy) setup requires 9 consecutive closes < close 4 bars earlier; bearish (sell) setup requires 9 consecutive closes > close 4 bars earlier. Setup is perfected when the low of bar 8 or 9 (buy) is ≤ the lows of bars 6 and 7, or the high of bar 8 or 9 (sell) is ≥ the highs of bars 6 and 7.

Countdown phase (13): after a completed setup, count bars where close ≤ low 2 bars earlier (buy) or close ≥ high 2 bars earlier (sell). Bars need not be consecutive. The 13-vs-8 qualifier requires bar 13's low ≤ bar 8's close (buy) or bar 13's high ≥ bar 8's close (sell); if not met, an unqualified 13 prints with Qual=No.

TDST = price extreme of the prior opposing setup, used as support (buy TDST) or resistance (sell TDST). Shown alongside current close so you can gauge whether the level is intact.

Composite quality score (0–100): Each candidate is scored on signal type (40 base for qualified 13s, 25 for perfected 9s, etc.), plus modifiers: freshness (+15 if completed today), bar efficiency (+5 for clean count, –5 for choppy), TDST confluence (+10 intact, +5 within 2%), ATR-normalized move (+10 for 1.5–3.5 ATR Goldilocks zone, –8 if parabolic), and penalties for whipsaw (opposing 9 within 10 bars: –15) or 25%+ recent moves (event-driven: –10). Liquidity floor: tickers with avg daily $-volume below $5M are excluded entirely. Star ratings: 5★ = 80+, 4★ = 65–79, 3★ = 50–64, 2★ = 35–49, 1★ = below 35.

Options selection (Top 10 picks): For each pick, the scanner fetches the full options chain via Polygon's options snapshot. Target DTE windows: 21–35 days for 9 setups, 35–60 days for qualified 13 countdowns, 28–45 days for unqualified 13s and active countdowns. The scanner prefers standard monthly expirations (3rd Friday). For each pick it surfaces 3 candidate strikes by delta: slight ITM (~0.65 delta), ATM (~0.50), slight OTM (~0.35). Buy signals get calls, sell signals get puts. Each contract is annotated with bid/ask, mid, implied volatility, open interest, day volume, and bid-ask spread %. IV is compared against 20-day historical volatility (annualized): IV/HV > 1.3 = "IV high vs HV" (options expensive), < 0.9 = "IV low vs HV" (options cheap). Contracts with OI < 100, volume < 10, or spread > 15% are flagged with warnings.

Earnings filter: When enabled, the scanner queries Polygon's /vX/reference/financials endpoint for each candidate ticker and excludes any whose most recent 10-Q/10-K filing was more than the configured threshold (default 70 days) ago. The logic: TD trades typically resolve in 1-4 weeks; if a stock hasn't reported in 70+ days, its next earnings is likely within your trade window. Filing date is a proxy for the earnings announcement date — they're typically within a week of each other but not identical. Q4 results often fold into the annual 10-K (gap can extend to 4-5 months), and companies with fiscal calendars that don't match calendar quarters may be incorrectly flagged. Always verify earnings dates in your broker before trading. Earnings dates are cached in browser localStorage for 7 days to minimize API calls.

Important caveats for options: Polygon's Options Starter is end-of-day data — bid/ask/IV shown are yesterday's close, not live quotes. Always verify in your broker before trading. Recommended DTE/strike framework is conventional practice for short-term technical trades, not a backtested edge.

Not implemented in this version: Recycle (R), Combo, the optional 8-vs-5 countdown deferral, intersection variants, weekly-timeframe confluence, and earnings-date filtering. The 13-vs-8 qualifier is implemented since it's standard.

Polygon endpoint used: /v2/aggs/ticker/{ticker}/range/1/day/{from}/{to}?adjusted=true
